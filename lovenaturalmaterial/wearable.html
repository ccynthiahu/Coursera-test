  • So Much to Love About Wearable Blankets/Sleep Sacks

During the baby's first year of life, the number one source of death is SIDS, Sudden Infant Death Syndrome (Nova Scotia Health, 2016). The exact cause is mysterious. The Mayo Clinic offers some plausible explanations.

Every parent and caregiver should know how to prevent that precious bundle of joy from being suffocated while sleeping. Besides placing the baby on his/her back and keeping the crib clear of comforter, pillow, bumper, and soft toys, it is important to have a firm mattress and sleep clothing or cover that would not in any way hinder the baby's breathing or overheat the baby (no sweat behind the neck, Nova Scotia Health).

Full bodysuits (onesies or footed onesies) seem to be the easiest and safest solution, if the room temperature is appropriate. When the baby does need a cover, drawing from generations of folk wisdom and browsing hundreds of products on the market, we believe that swaddles and sleep sacks, or wearable blankets, are good choices to keep the little angels cozy, warm, and safe. Bodysuits, as the inner layer or pajama, could combine with sleep sacks. We think that it may be a good idea to stick to bodysuits and/or wearable blankets for a couple of years.
