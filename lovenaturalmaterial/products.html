Swaddles and Wearable Blankets/Sleep Sacks

During the baby's first year of life, the number one source of death is SIDS, Sudden Infant Death Syndrome (Nova Scotia Health, 2016). The exact cause is mysterious. The Mayo Clinic offers some plausible explanations. If you are interested, this is the link.

SIDS is serious enough that every parent and caregiver should know how to prevent that precious bundle of joy from being suffocated while sleeping. Besides placing the baby on his/her back and keeping the crib clear of comforter, pillow, bumper, and soft toys, it is important to have a firm mattress and sleep cover or clothing that would not in any way hinder the baby's breathing.

Of course the baby can wear a bodysuit, which is seemingly the safest solution, if the temperature of the bedroom is appropriate. When the baby does need a cover, the Loving Care series, parenting guides compiled by the Nova Scotia Health Authority (2016), suggested knitted or crocheted blankets. Drawing from generations of folk wisdom and browsing hundreds of products on the market, we believe that swaddles and sleep sacks, or wearable blankets, are good choices to keep the little angels cozy, warm, and most importantly safe. Depending on the development of the baby, we think that it is a good idea to stick to bodysuits and/or wearable blankets for a couple of years.

In a little bit we will list examples of swaddles and wearable blankets that are cotton, cotton muslin, or wool you can order online.

Newborns may like to be swaddled, as it feels like being back in the womb. Before the baby can turn or wave his/her arms and legs strongly, a baby up to a few months old may still be swaddled, particularly when using a commercial swaddle that is designed to have some leeway in its tie and provide ample room for the little legs. Often it works to choose clothing a little bigger than fits the baby for him/her to grow into it. However, we found in our experience that it does not work this way in case of swaddles, which ideally should fit perfect and relatively snug.

If you have a cotton blanket that is the right thickness and size, you can certainly make a swaddle yourself. Here is a video demonstration by Mount Sinai Parenting Center.

You also have the option to buy a couple of swaddles that are even much easier than making a swaddle yourself.
