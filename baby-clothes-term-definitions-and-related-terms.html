Baby Clothes Term Definitions and Related Terms

Before we talk about the fun things about how to dress your baby, it makes sense to define relevant terms, so that we will be on the same page as we write and you read this website. You may or may not have noticed that there are jargons in the sphere of baby clothes, which are not to be confused with adult clothes terms that are similar. To further confuse things, the same item may be called with different terms and one term may not mean the same thing. First of all, we will define baby clothes terms. Most of them refer to baby sleep clothes. We will provide details with photos and links in the next article, How to dress your baby for sleep. Then we will distinguish baby, newborn, infant, and toddler from the point of view of baby clothes. Finally we will define TOG rating, a term that indicates the warmth of blankets and sleep sacks. Baby Clothes Term Definitions

Bodysuit:

A special kind of T-shirt with flaps at the bottom and most often three snaps over the crotch. It may be long-sleeve, short-sleeve, or sleeveless. Most bodysuits feature an envelope design, with overlaps at the shoulders, so that the neckline can be opened wide to go through the baby's head, which should be well supported at all times. The other term for bodysuit is onesie. Note that onesie is a patented term, owned by Gerber Children’s Wear. The Amazon seller forum includes a discussion, where people credit Gerber for coming up with this clever design that makes diaper change much easier. In North American slang, it may be called a jumper.

Romper:

One-piece outer garment. When designed for a baby, it can be similar to a bodysuit, with snaps over the crotch. But it is fancier than a bodysuit, with extra length over the legs, either like shorts or pants or with elastics along the hems that can create a bell shape.

Sleeper:

One-piece pajamas that include a long zipper or line of snaps down the front till the cuff of one leg or snaps down the inside of both legs. It is obviously designed with diaper change in mind. They come with feet (footed sleeper or footie) or without feet (footless sleeper). They are also called sleep 'n play, which aptly describes the multifunctional feature of this convenient one-piece design. In online listings, these are sometimes called baby coverall, overall, jumpsuit, or sleepsuit.

Sleeper gown:
Amazon

Similar to sleep sacks, but it tends to be long-sleeve, has no zipper, but instead has an elastic gathering or extra long flaps at the bottom. Sleeper gowns on the market are mostly for infants up to about six months old. The elastic gathering allows for pull-up at the time of diaper change, and the long flaps can be tied into a knot or untied for diaper change. It is also called nightgown or simply gown.

Swaddle:

"Burrito" or "cocoon" that wraps the baby. It can be handmade with love from a blanket or commercially produced with a closure by Velcro, hook and loop, or zipper.

Wearable blanket:

Sleeping bag that can be worn over the shoulder with either openings or sleeves for the arms. Most designs are sleeveless with a long two-way zipper down the front, down the side, or diagonally. The zipper can be opened up to release the baby or opened down for diaper change. It is also called sleep sack or the generic sleep bag or sleeping bag.

Newborn, Infant, Toddler, Baby

Here we will quickly define newborn, infant, toddler, and baby from the perspective of baby clothes. Newborn means a baby from birth to about 3 or 4 months, before he/she can turn or roll. This is a period of time when swaddling may work well. Starting when the baby is able to turn till about one year of age, a baby is also called an infant. When the baby starts toddling, at about one year, he/she is called a toddler, until around 5. Normally baby clothes size T goes from 2T-5T. Baby is a catch-all term that includes newborn, infant, and toddler.

TOG Rating on Baby Clothes

TOG ratings can help select sleep covers. What is TOG? It stands for Thermal Overall Grade and measures the warmth of bed covers (Cambridge Dictionary). TOG ratings on baby clothing are on a different system than adult duvets. A winter sleep sack for a baby measures TOG 2.5 or 3.5, including its padding or insulation. The higher, the warmer. TOG 1.0 or lower are normally for the spring or summer. Products on the market do not often include the rating. This is where an adult's life experience comes in. Also check the baby's body temperature (using the back of the neck test or a thermometer) to establish his/her own standards.
